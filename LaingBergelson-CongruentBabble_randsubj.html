The remaining 17 infants showed signs of a developing VMS consonant at 0;10: a given consonant was dominant in the session (defined here as â‰¥20 tokens of the same consonant accounting for >20% of overall consonant production, cf.Â Athari, Wang, Dey & Rvachew, 2019), but did not reach our initial criteria of â‰¥50 tokens (mean n=29.5, SD = 8.64, R=20-48; Mean %=.50, SD=.17, R=.23-.9). Infants who showed low/high production at 0;11 were then grouped as noVMS (n=6) or withVMS (n=8), respectively, as per our initial criteria. Finally, three infants were consistent in producing the same consonant stably at 0;10 and 0;11 (â‰¥20 productions of a consonant type, accounting for >20% of all consonant tokens at both 0;10 and 0;11), thereby demonstrating â€˜well- practiced and longitudinally stable vocal productionsâ€™ (McCune & Vihman, 2001, p.671), and were also categorized as withVMS.
